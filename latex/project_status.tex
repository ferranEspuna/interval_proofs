\begin{abstract}
We study the largest possible measure of a measurable set $A\subset\T$ for
which
\[
    (A+A)\cap mA=\varnothing,
\]
equivalently $0\notin A+A-mA$.  This document is the central record of the
project.  It separates proved results, finite-dimensional computations, and
open directions; includes the existing detailed proofs without duplicating
their contents; and records the literature route through inverse sumset
theorems.  The principal completed result is the sharp $1/5$ bound for a
union of two intervals when $m=3$.  For general $m$, an explicit equally
spaced construction has density
\(
\lfloor (m+2)^2/8\rfloor/(m(m+2))>1/8
\), while the best uniform upper bound used here is $1/3.1955$.
\end{abstract}
